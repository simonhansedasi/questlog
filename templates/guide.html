Quick Start Guide

From account to living world — five steps.

What RippleForge does

RippleForge tracks your story world as a graph of events and relationships. When something happens to a character, every connected group and person feels it automatically — that's the ripple system.

After a scene or session, paste your notes into the AI parser. It extracts discrete world events, lets you review them, then commits them — firing ripples to every connected character in one go.

The Narrator dashboard is your intel layer: hot characters under pressure, active tensions, stale threads, and a "What happens next?" projection based purely on your world state — no hallucination, just consequence.

1

Create your world

From your home screen, choose Start from Scratch (blank world) or a starter template. Give it a name and set a Narrator PIN — you'll need the PIN every time you access the Narrator dashboard.

The Narrator PIN is separate from your account password. It's what you type at the Narrator login screen inside a world. Write it down somewhere.

2

Add your characters and groups

Go to World and add the key players — the characters your story revolves around and the factions or groups pulling the strings. Names and a brief role are enough to start.

Don't declare relationships yet. Relationships emerge from events you log. Add Steve, add Cheryl — RippleForge will figure out what they are to each other once they start interacting.

3

Log their first interaction — watch a relationship form

From the Narrator dashboard, use the Quick Log. Pick Who this affects, then set Caused by. Add a note and a tone — positive, negative, or neutral. Log it.

The rule: think subject → object

Caused by = who did it.  Who this affects = who it landed on.
Memory hook: "[Caused by] did it. It happened to [Who this affects]."

Worked example — Steve & Cheryl (neighborhood drama)

Beat 1 — Steve moves in
No actor. Who this affects: Steve. Caused by: nobody. Tone: neutral. A world event — no score yet.
Beat 2 — Cheryl brings cookies; Steve refuses to chat
Log this as two entries — one per side of the interaction:
• Who this affects: Cheryl / Caused by: Steve / tone: negative — the snub landed on Cheryl.
• Who this affects: Steve / Caused by: Cheryl / tone: neutral — Cheryl made a gesture Steve didn't want.
Beat 3 — Cheryl anonymously files a complaint
Who this affects: Steve / Caused by: Cheryl / tone: negative. Mark Narrator-only if Steve doesn't know who filed it — the event exists and Cheryl caused it, but Steve only sees the consequence.

After a few interactions RippleForge starts computing a relationship score between them. A pattern solidifies: ally, hostile, friendly. Open Steve's character page and you'll see it.

When the relationship is clear, you'll see a Formalize prompt on the character page. Accepting it adds a structural graph edge — this is what enables the ripple system to automatically propagate events between them. Until then, the score is still there, just not yet wired into the propagation network.

Tip: Go to World → Ripples after formalizing to see the full propagation trace — every downstream effect from a single logged event.

4

After a scene or session: parse your notes

On the Narrator dashboard, paste your raw notes into the Notes field and hit Parse into Events ✦. The AI extracts discrete world events — one per character or group — and presents them for your review.

Your notes can be rough. Bullet points, sentence fragments, shorthand — it handles all of it. You're reviewing the output, not the input.

Review the proposed entries. Edit any you want to change. Uncheck anything that doesn't belong. Then hit Commit Selected — events log and ripples fire in one step.

Tip: The parser also suggests relationship edges implied by your notes. Accept them to backfill ripples across the full event history for that connection.

5

Before your next scene: check the intel layer

The top of your Narrator dashboard is the World State panel — hot characters under pressure, active risks, stale threads that haven't moved in a while. This is computed from your event history, not manually curated.

Hit What happens next? ✦ to generate consequence projections — what the world does if left unchecked. Review them, commit the ones you plan to run with, and they'll appear in your world as Pending Projections until they play out.

Hit Brief me ✦ in the Session Plan card to auto-generate a pre-session brief from your world state — pending futures, active threads, hot characters, protagonist stakes. Use it as a starting point for your notes.

Key concepts

Ripple An automatic event fired to a related character when you log something to their ally or rival. Strength decays with relational distance and weight.
Relationship score Computed from event history, not manually set. Any two characters who interact via the "Caused by" field accumulate a score automatically. Decays over time if nothing is logged.
Relationship edge A formal connection between two characters that enables ripple propagation. Emerges naturally from interactions — when the pattern is clear, RippleForge prompts you to formalize it.
Narrator-only Events marked narrator-only are hidden from collaborators even if they have access to the world. Use for secrets, hidden motives, and plot threads not yet revealed.
Pending Projection A committed "What happens next" entry. Confirm it when it plays out (fires ripples), or dismiss it (marks protagonist intervention).
Narrator PIN Separate from your account password. Required to access the Narrator dashboard within a world. Share the world URL without the PIN to give collaborators read-only access.
